        <h2 class="section-title">Selected Work</h2>

        

        <div class="project-card">

            <h3>Chest X-Ray Classification System</h3>

            <div>

                <span class="project-tag">Machine Learning</span>

                <span class="project-tag">Healthcare</span>

                <span class="project-tag">Image Classification</span>

            </div>

            

            <div class="project-section">

                <h4>The Challenge</h4>

                <p>Radiologists face time pressure when reviewing chest X-rays to identify pneumonia cases. Misdiagnosis or delays can have serious consequences for patient outcomes. How might we leverage machine learning to support faster, more accurate pneumonia detection?</p>

            </div>

            

            <div class="project-section">

                <h4>My Role & Approach</h4>

                <p>As part of a data science team, I developed and fine-tuned a deep learning model using transfer learning techniques. The project involved:</p>

                <ul>

                    <li>Preprocessing and organizing a dataset of chest X-ray images</li>

                    <li>Implementing EfficientNetV2S, a pre-trained convolutional neural network</li>

                    <li>Fine-tuning the model to optimize performance with limited computational resources</li>

                    <li>Evaluating classification accuracy using precision, recall, and F1-score metrics</li>

                </ul>

            </div>

            

            <div class="project-section">

                <h4>Technical Implementation</h4>

                <div class="tech-stack">

                    <span class="tech-badge">Python</span>

                    <span class="tech-badge">TensorFlow</span>

                    <span class="tech-badge">Keras</span>

                    <span class="tech-badge">Google Colab</span>

                    <span class="tech-badge">EfficientNetV2S</span>

                </div>

                <p style="margin-top: 1.5rem;">The model was trained for 30 epochs with early stopping implemented at epoch 15 to prevent overfitting. Transfer learning allowed us to achieve strong results without extensive computational resources.</p>

            </div>

            

            <div class="project-section">

                <h4>Results & Impact</h4>

                <p>The classification system achieved strong performance across key metrics:</p>

                <div class="metrics-grid">

                    <div class="metric-box">

                        <div class="metric-value">93.9%</div>

                        <div class="metric-label">Overall Accuracy</div>

                    </div>

                    <div class="metric-box">

                        <div class="metric-value">95.4%</div>

                        <div class="metric-label">Pneumonia Recall</div>

                    </div>

                    <div class="metric-box">

                        <div class="metric-value">91.5%</div>

                        <div class="metric-label">Normal Recall</div>

                    </div>

                    <div class="metric-box">

                        <div class="metric-value">93.5%</div>

                        <div class="metric-label">F1 Score</div>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <p style="margin-top: 1.5rem;">The high pneumonia recall rate (95.4%) is particularly important in a clinical context, as it minimizes false negatives—cases where pneumonia is present but not detected.</p>

            </div>

            

            <div class="project-section">

                <h4>Key Learnings</h4>

                <ul>

                    <li>Transfer learning can produce robust results even with limited computational resources</li>

                    <li>Model performance must be evaluated in context—high recall for disease detection is critical in healthcare applications</li>

                    <li>Early stopping and careful validation prevent overfitting and ensure model generalizability</li>
